## Needs Decision

These items should not block all progress, but they need a human choice before scaling.

- Public home for the demo page: GitHub Pages, existing website, or private share link.
- Contact identity: which sender name and email should appear on the page and outreach.
- First wedge: ecommerce inventory/margin or SaaS churn/support.
- First offer: one-off report, monthly reporting prep, or assisted demo report.
- CTA wording: "See an example report" or "Send a messy export."

Recommended defaults:

- Start with ecommerce inventory/margin.
- Use "See an example report" as the first CTA.
- Offer a one-off example/report review before talking about recurring software.
- Keep the first outreach batch to 5 messages.

## First 7 Days

Day 1:

- Finalize the demo page contact info.
- Choose the first 5 leads.
- Prepare one approved outreach message.

Day 2:

- Send the first 5 manual messages.
- Publish or share one ecommerce proof-point post.
- Log everything in the trackers.

Day 3:

- Review replies, clicks, comments, or silence.
- Adjust only the first sentence of the outreach if needed.
- Do not rewrite the whole strategy yet.

Day 4:

- Send 5 more messages if the first 5 were clean.
- Capture objections exactly as written.

Day 5:

- Compare ecommerce vs SaaS signal if both were tested.
- Pick the next 10 leads based on real response quality.

Day 6:

- Turn the best example into a one-page PDF or cleaner public page.
- Add the safety/scope note near the CTA.

Day 7:

- Decide whether to continue, narrow the segment, or change the offer.
- Write a short weekly scorecard.

## Weekly Scorecard

Track only these numbers at first:

- Manual messages sent.
- Meaningful replies.
- Example report requests.
- Objections.
- Best-performing segment.
- Best-performing phrase.
- Follow-up tasks.

Ignore vanity metrics until there are real replies.

## Guardrails

- Do not bulk email.
- Do not buy paid tools yet.
- Do not target medical companies.
- Do not imply synthetic demo numbers are customer results.
- Do not claim a company has a problem. Ask whether the problem exists.
- Do not scale until there are 5-10 careful manual touches and some learning.
