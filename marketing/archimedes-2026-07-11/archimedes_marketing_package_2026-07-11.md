# Archimedes Marketing Package - 2026-07-11

## Working Positioning

Archimedes turns messy operational data into decision-ready reports: upload a real business dataset, choose the target question, and get a focused action brief with risks, segments, and next steps.

Best near-term wedge: ecommerce and SaaS teams that already live in exports, merchant reports, Shopify/app data, retention data, and spreadsheet cleanup.

Avoid sounding like: another generic "chat with your CSV" tool.

Sound like: a practical analyst that cleans up the mess, finds the operating issue, and produces a usable report.

## Social Posts

### Post 1 - Ecommerce Proof Point

Messy ecommerce data usually hides the operating issue.

In one Archimedes demo, a 7,200-row SKU-week dataset showed:

- 37.2% stockout rate
- $3.0M estimated lost revenue
- 851 promo-heavy rows
- Stockouts concentrated around discount-led demand

The recommendation was not "make a dashboard."

It was: stop launching 20%+ promos unless inventory covers at least 1.25x forecast demand.

That is the kind of report Archimedes is built for.

### Post 2 - SaaS Churn Angle

Support tickets are not always engagement.

In a SaaS churn demo, accounts with 8+ tickets in 90 days churned at 29.7%, compared with 8.9% for lower-ticket accounts.

The useful output was a rescue queue:

- 8+ tickets
- 2+ unresolved issues
- onboarding score under 55
- long time-to-value

Archimedes is designed to turn messy customer data into this kind of action list.

### Post 3 - Category Differentiation

There are plenty of tools that let you chat with a CSV.

Archimedes is pointed at a different job:

Take messy business exports and turn them into a focused operating report.

What happened?
Where is risk concentrated?
Which segment should the team act on first?
What rule should change next week?

That is the gap between "interesting analysis" and useful work.

### Post 4 - Founder Build Update

Recent Archimedes testing ran structured datasets through upload, target-aware reporting, and model training workflows.

The useful part was not just that the workflow completed.

The useful part was seeing which business problems produced clear action:

- churn and retention

- credit/default risk
- fraud/cost-utilization review

The near-term marketing wedge is ecommerce/SaaS because the examples and lead list are already aligned.
