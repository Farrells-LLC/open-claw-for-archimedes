# Archimedes Company Scale Roadmap - 2026-07-11

## North Star

Become the practical first-pass analyst for messy operational data.

Archimedes should help teams move from raw exports to a concrete decision faster than they can with spreadsheets, dashboards, or generic CSV chat.

## 30-Day Objective

Validate whether ecommerce/SaaS teams have a painful, recurring messy-export workflow and whether a focused action report is compelling enough to earn replies, calls, or sample data.

## Week 1 - Proof and Outreach

Complete:

- Polish the demo share page.
- Choose the public report URL.
- Send 5-10 manual first-batch messages.
- Publish 2-3 proof-point posts.
- Track replies, report requests, and objections.

Success signal:

- At least 2 meaningful replies or example-report requests from 10 careful touches.

## Week 2 - Learn and Narrow

Complete:

- Rewrite the one-liner based on actual replies.
- Split ecommerce analytics vs Shopify apps vs B2B ecommerce SaaS response rates.
- Add one more demo if the winning segment points somewhere specific.
- Turn the best reply language into website copy.

Success signal:

- One segment produces clearer pain language than the others.

## Week 3 - Offer Design

Complete:

- Draft 2-3 packaging options:
  - one-off action report
  - monthly export cleanup/reporting pass
  - demo dataset/report preparation for SaaS vendors
- Create a simple intake form for messy export submissions.
- Define what data is accepted and what is out of bounds.

Success signal:

- A prospect says what they would pay for or asks how to start.

## Week 4 - Repeatable Motion

Complete:

- Build a 50-touch manual pipeline from the lead list.
- Create a weekly reporting cadence.
- Save objections and winning phrases.
- Decide whether to build product features, more examples, or a service wrapper first.

Success signal:

- The team knows which wedge to pursue next and has enough signal to avoid random marketing.

## Operating Metrics

Track weekly:

- Manual touches sent.
- Reply rate.
- Positive reply rate.
- Example report requests.
- Calls booked.
- Sample datasets offered.
- Qualified opportunities.
- Common objections.
- Best-performing subject line/post.

## Strategic Bets

Bet 1:

The buyer does not wake up wanting "AI analytics." They wake up annoyed by messy files, late reports, unclear risks, and manual prep.

Bet 2:

The most valuable output is not a chart. It is a priority queue, rule, or next operating decision.

Bet 3:

Ecommerce/SaaS is the right first wedge because the lead list, demo assets, and non-medical examples already line up.

Bet 4:

The company can scale by productizing a repeatable analyst workflow before trying to become a general-purpose BI platform.

## Risks

- The market may perceive Archimedes as generic CSV AI unless the message stays concrete.
- Demo examples may not transfer to real messy customer files without strong intake limits.
- Outbound could produce false negatives if the public demo link is not polished.
- Regulated industries can distract from the safer ecommerce/SaaS wedge.

## Next Assets To Build

- Public demo URL.
- One-page PDF version of the ecommerce example.
- Intake form questions for messy exports.
- Objection tracker.
- Simple case-study format for future real users.
- "What data can I send?" safety page.
