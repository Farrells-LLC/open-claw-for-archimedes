# Archimedes Sales Enablement - 2026-07-11

## ICP For The First Manual Campaign

Best fit:

- Ecommerce analytics platforms.
- Shopify apps and Shopify partner teams.
- B2B ecommerce SaaS companies.
- SaaS/customer-success teams with churn, onboarding, support, or health-score exports.
- Operators who already use spreadsheets to prepare reports, demos, or customer-facing insights.

Poor fit for now:

- Medical companies or regulated healthcare outreach.
- Teams asking for fully governed enterprise BI.
- Prospects who only want dashboards and already have clean data pipelines.
- Anyone expecting guaranteed savings or automated decisions without review.

## Qualification Questions

- Do customer, order, SKU, or support exports take manual cleanup before anyone can use them?
- Are demo datasets or reporting files slowing the team down?
- Is the problem recurring enough that a faster first-pass report would be valuable?
- Does the team need an action queue or operating rule, not just a chart?
- Would a synthetic example report help them evaluate fit before sharing real data?

## Discovery Script

Short opener:

I am trying to learn whether messy exports are a real bottleneck for ecommerce and SaaS teams. Do reporting prep, demo datasets, or customer/SKU exports take more cleanup than they should?

If yes:

That is the problem Archimedes is aimed at. It turns a business dataset into a focused action report: what happened, where the risk is, and what the team should review first.

If they ask for an example:

The ecommerce demo finds stockout and margin leakage from SKU-week data. The SaaS demo turns support pressure into a churn rescue queue. Both use synthetic, non-medical data.

If they are not interested:

No problem at all. I appreciate the reply. If you ever hit a messy-export reporting problem later, I can send an example.

## Objection Handling

"We already have BI."

That makes sense. Archimedes is not meant to replace a mature BI stack. It is more useful before data reaches that stage: messy exports, demo datasets, ad hoc reporting prep, or one-off operating questions that need a fast first pass.

"Can ChatGPT do this?"

For simple files, sometimes. The difference we are testing is workflow: guided target selection, structured reporting, specific action queues, and reusable operating logic rather than a one-off chat answer.

"We cannot share customer data."

Totally fair. The first step can be a synthetic or anonymized sample, or I can send a demo report to show the format before any data discussion.

"Is this a dashboard?"

Not primarily. Dashboards are useful once the metric system is known. Archimedes is more useful when the team has raw rows and needs to figure out what issue deserves attention.

"What does it cost?"

We are still validating the best packaging. Right now I am looking for fit and feedback from teams that have recurring messy-export/reporting problems.

## Demo Talk Track

1. "Here is the kind of raw operating problem Archimedes is built for."
2. "In the ecommerce demo, the issue is not just stockouts; it is discount-led demand creating stockouts before promotion planning catches up."

4. "In the SaaS demo, high support volume becomes a churn rescue queue."
5. "The goal is to get from rows to a reviewable decision faster."
